Secret Pigeon

We use end-to-end encryption using Public-Private key cryptography. This means security without having to distribute encryption keys yourself and without having to trust other parties. Instead of using 3rd party JavaScript code we only use the high-quality API’s provided by modern browsers. As your secrets are encrypted in your browser before being sent, we never see them, and can’t leak them. Source code is available for anyone to review and is served directly by GitHub. Hashes are used to verify message integrity and to prevent tampering. As an extra measure your data is also transferred over TLS and further encrypted using AES when waiting in our data center.

We are different

Other popular secret sharing sites either don’t support end to end encryption or require you to provide your own encryption keys, which then must be shared with your intended recipient; leaving you back where you started. Even if you trust owners of the site to transfer your secrets without peeking there is still the potential risk of information being leaked accidentally from the site due to bugs or hacks, possibly without anybody being aware. Features such as links which only work once don’t do anything to mitigate these risks as you still can’t know that your secret was securely removed from all servers and logs.

Story

During World War II the British intelligence service air dropped carrier pigeons across occupied Europe. These pigeons came with instructions for resistance groups to record encrypted intelligence reports. The reports were attached to the pigeons which flew them safely across enemy lines and back to allied command.
